Strengthening Our Commitment to Ethical, Quality-Driven Business

B Corp certification is not a marketing credential. It is a structured commitment to a specific standard of business conduct, one that requires ongoing verification, transparent reporting, and a genuine willingness to be held accountable for how your business impacts the people and communities it touches.

What B Corp Means for Anywise

We sought B Corp certification because it aligns with what we already believed about how technology companies operating in high-stakes environments should conduct themselves. Defence and government technology is not a neutral domain. The platforms we build affect operational decisions that have real consequences for people. The ethical standards we apply to that work matter.

B Corp certification requires us to meet rigorous standards across five dimensions: governance, workers, community, environment, and customers. It requires us to report on those standards openly and to have that reporting independently verified. For a company that builds tools for decision-making environments, operating with the same transparency we advocate for in others is not optional. It is essential.

Ethical Technology in Practice

For Anywise, ethical technology is not a constraint on capability. It is a design requirement. We build explainability into our analytical platforms because operators need to understand why a recommendation was made, not just what it was. We maintain sovereign deployment capability because data sovereignty is a legitimate operational requirement. We design for the humans who use our tools, not just for the systems they connect to.

  • Explainable AI: every recommendation includes a transparent evidence chain
  • Data sovereignty: deployed on Australian-owned infrastructure
  • Human-centred design: tools built for the operators who use them
  • Continuous accountability: B Corp verification and independent reporting

We see our B Corp status as a foundation, not a destination. The standards will evolve, our business will grow, and the challenges of operating ethically in complex environments will continue to require active attention. We are committed to meeting that challenge, and to being transparent about where we are falling short as well as where we are succeeding.
